Avoid the following AI writing tics:

"Delve into" / "Dive deeper": Often used to transition into a more detailed explanation.
"In today's fast-paced world" / "In the modern digital landscape": Classic, sweeping opening phrases.
"It is important to note that": A filler phrase used to add pseudo-importance.
"Revolutionize" / "Cutting-edge" / "Innovative": Overused buzzwords intended to add energy.
"Tapestry" / "Leverage" / "Nuance" / "Echoes": Words that sound sophisticated but are overused, often in combinations like "rich tapestry". 

Structural and Rhetorical Patterns
"It's not just X, it's Y" / "Negative Parallelism": Using negation for artificial drama (e.g., "It's not just a tool—it's a transformation").
The "Rule of Three": Grouping everything in lists of three (e.g., "Clear, concise, and compelling").
Excessive Em Dashes (—): Using dashes instead of commas or periods to connect ideas.
