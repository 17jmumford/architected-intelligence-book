Chapter 4: AI unlocks the potential of untapped data

Untapped data

Core ideas

  • The usual barrier is not “more intelligence” but information architecture connecting operational reality to strategic answers.
  • Use the Disconnected Command Center metaphor: a “Source Floor” of messy unstructured data and a “Value Ceiling” of decisions that stay dark until you route signal upward.
  • Human “switchboard operators” scale poorly; aim to automate routing while elevating experts to architects of the system.
  • Shift from traditional Information Systems to Knowledge Systems: governance that makes data trustworthy for people and machines.
  • Collect breaks into prioritize (what knowledge to pursue) and acquire (capture or create documentation with meaningful insight).
  • Score unstructured candidates with performance delta, creation/distribution/maintenance cost, and shelf life, and avoid hoard-everything traps.
  • The best proprietary signal often lives with subject-matter experts who still need development and incentives to capture knowledge well.

Principles from the chapter

  • Greater care should be given to unstructured data as it usually spoils more quickly than traditional data.
  • Automating the transfer of knowledge is the holy grail of AI empowerment and should be one of the primary ways you drive value with AI integration.
  • Unstructured data pays off only when it reaches the right people, in the right form, at the right moment.
  • The better your examples from both a completeness and coverage perspective, the better your AI-first process will perform.
  • The most valuable unstructured data often lives in the heads of subject-matter experts who understand the work, fine-grained detail, and pain points at a microscopic level.
