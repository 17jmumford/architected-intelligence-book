Style guide based on the book cover

## Colors
The entire palette is built on two anchors — a warm golden amber and near-black — against a clean white/off-white background. The exact primary gold is #E8A020, with a lighter tint at #F5C95A and a very pale background tint at #FFF3DC. The robot arm illustration uses a distinct warm sand tone at #FAD38A. Text runs from #1A1A1A (headlines) to #4A4A4A (body) to #888 (labels/captions). Background surfaces use #F7F6F3 (slightly warm off-white) and pure #FFFFFF for cards.
## Typography
The headline typeface is a high-contrast serif — Georgia or a similar transitional serif (think Playfair Display, Libre Baskerville) — set very large and tight with line-height: 1.05. This is the dominant visual element. The subtitle and all UI text is a clean sans-serif set in gold. Author bylines and labels use uppercase tracking (letter-spacing: 0.1em). Never mix gold text with the serif headlines — they live in separate layers.
## Illustration System
There's a deliberate two-mode visual language: human elements are drawn as outlines only (no fill, 1.5px stroke, dark), while machine/AI elements are solid gold fills. This is the central metaphor of the brand — use it consistently in icons, section headers, and hero graphics.
## Key Principles for the Website
- White space is structural — the cover's generous margins should inform every section's padding
- Gold is used sparingly as an accent, not a background color
- The serif + gold combo is the "brand moment" — reserve it for hero/feature areas, not UI chrome
- Cards use a subtle 0.5px border in a warm gray (#E0DDD6), not harsh black borders
- Featured cards get a 1.5px solid #E8A020 border (no colored background fill)
